### 📝 Project Overview
This repository contains a web blog entry for **"THE DIGITAL VILLAGE: WEB DESIGN COMPETITION"** held at **Bapu Primary-High School**. 

The competition challenged students from Class 7 and 8 to apply their knowledge of **HTML and CSS** to create a digital narrative about rural roots and heritage. Prajath's project highlights the misty hills and biodiversity of Sakleshpur. The blog focuses on the unique "Malnad" culture, traditional architecture like the Manjarabad Fort, and the famous local cuisine of the region.

### 💻 Technical Skills Demonstrated
* **HTML5 Semantic Structure:** Excellent use of `<section>` tags to categorize different aspects of the travelogue, along with structured lists to present local delicacies and travel tips.
* **CSS3 Styling:** * **Layout Architecture:** Implementation of a 60% width centered blog box using `margin: auto`, ensuring a modern and readable content flow.
    * **Visual UI Design:** Consistent use of `border-radius` (10px for the container, 8px for images) to maintain the competition's design standards.
    * **Color Theory:** Chosen a Slate Gray background (`#55767C`) with a Dark Green content box (`#006400`), perfectly mirroring the "Green Route" and forest themes of Sakleshpur.
    * **Interactive Elements:** Integration of an external anchor link (`<a>`) to the official Sakleshpur Wikipedia page for further reading.
* **Content Curation:** Comprehensive coverage of local geography, historical sites (Manjarabad Fort, Belur, Halebidu), and specialized regional food like **Akki Rotti** and **Kadubu**.
